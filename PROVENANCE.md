# Provenance

## What Polymath authored

The following content was created by ACLAS College specifically for the Polymath dataset:

- **Course names, descriptions, learning outcomes, and tags** — synthesized from standard university curricula across multiple regions
- **Discipline definitions and ISCED-F/CIP mappings** — compiled from official UNESCO ISCED-F 2013 and NCES CIP 2020 classifications
- **Skill definitions, categories, and career links** — curated from industry job descriptions and skill frameworks
- **Career profiles, salary bands, and growth outlooks** — synthesized from BLS OOH, O*NET, and industry reports
- **Degree program compositions** — modeled on typical degree requirements at accredited US and European institutions
- **Prerequisite relationships between courses** — based on standard academic prerequisites observed across institutions
- **Framework level mappings** — derived from official EQF, AQF, and US qualification framework documentation

## Design influences

We acknowledge Marble Skill Taxonomy (https://github.com/withmarbleapp/os-taxonomy) as an inspiration for the open taxonomy concept. Polymath applies this paradigm to the fundamentally different domain of global higher education, with a richer multi-dimensional data model.

## Disclaimer

Polymath is a reference taxonomy, not an official accreditation or equivalency tool. Course names, credit mappings, and degree requirements are representative of common academic practice and should not be substituted for official institutional policies or government qualification assessments.
